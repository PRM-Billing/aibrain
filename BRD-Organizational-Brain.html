AI Operating Platform (top-level) → Nine specialized assistants: Executive Decision, Document Pipeline, Finance, Operations, Knowledge & Retrieval Live — nine assistants operational Living corporate intelligence layer
Executive Summary: This initiative delivers an AI Operating Platform that captures real operating patterns from meetings, decisions, workflows, and institutional knowledge — not one-off summaries. The platform is the top-level business system; specialized assistants for executive decisions, business case and requirements documents, finance analysis, operations, people onboarding, knowledge retrieval, process documentation, and backlog planning are modules inside that platform. Phase 1 (capture, structure, and human approval) is live today; later phases add deeper memory, hybrid retrieval, SME streams, and executive-grade decision support.
Presentation narrative: Present the AI Operating Platform first, then drill into assistants on demand. Example path: "How do you make decisions?" → Executive Decision Assistant · "How do you turn meetings into approved documents?" → Document Pipeline (meeting → business case → requirements) · "How do departments and finance plug in?" → Operations and Finance assistants with shared memory and retrieval. Current State vs. Target State

Current (Fragmented)

  • Institutional knowledge trapped in meetings, chat, and individual memory
  • Repeated rediscovery of decisions, rationale, and process logic
  • High bus factor; weak continuity when people are unavailable or leave
  • Tools skew to transcription or static docs — not compounding intelligence
  • Single monolithic prompt handles all pipeline steps → context-window limits cause silent step omission ("survival mode"); stale prompt files compound this
  • Recordings require manual download/conversion before processing — no automated ingest path

Target (AI Operating Platform)

  • Systematic ingest → extract → validate → store with source traceability
  • Searchable knowledge objects linked to people, topics, processes, decisions
  • Governed lifecycle: draft vs trusted; versioning and audit for compliance
  • Progressive intelligence: retrieval today; pattern & decision support tomorrow
  • Whole-team multi-user access (Contributor / Reviewer / Approver); each pipeline step is an independent node — no monolithic single-call execution
  • Recordings auto-picked from Google Drive / OneDrive / email — no manual handoff before pipeline triggers

Business / Functional Requirements
ID Priority Requirement
BR-OB-01 High Ingestion: Accept meeting audio (transcribed via OpenAI Whisper / compatible ASR path), existing transcripts, operational notes, prompts, and linked documents with metadata (date, participants, department, sensitivity).
BR-OB-02 High Structured extraction: Produce machine-readable artifacts (JSON) beyond free-text summaries — decisions, action items, owners, deadlines, risks, open questions, process changes.
BR-OB-03 High Knowledge architecture: Categorize and tag outputs by domain, topic, role, process, and decision type; support stable identifiers and cross-links between related knowledge objects.
BR-OB-04 Med Retention & memory: Persist structured knowledge with versioning; distinguish draft, reviewed, and trusted states; retain source pointers to original input.
BR-OB-05 Med Retrieval: Enable search and Q&A over approved knowledge with citations; phase hybrid retrieval (semantic + structured / graph) as corpus grows.
BR-OB-06 Med Decision support: Surface prior decisions, rationale, and constraints when similar topics recur; flag conflicts with newer information (human adjudication).
BR-OB-07 High Governance (multi-user): Role-based contribute/validate/approve for the whole team (Himani, Luis, Javier team, Avinasha — not single-user); audit trail for reads/writes; configurable retention and export for legal review.
BR-OB-08 Med SME expansion: Allow department leaders to submit structured knowledge streams into the same taxonomy with delegated approval workflows.
BR-OB-09 High Orchestration / node model: Each pipeline action (ingest, ASR, extract, score, publish) is an independent module/node with an explicit contract; a thin orchestrator (LangGraph or equivalent) encodes only order + routing rules. Existing tools — AutoDoc, CC call analyzer, and the meeting workflow — are nodes in the same graph. Prevents "survival mode" step-dropping caused by monolithic single-call execution.
BR-OB-10 High Prompt/config freshness: Cue files, context files, and schema definitions must be re-read from disk per invocation — never recalled from conversation thread history. Each run starts as a fresh agent window with the plan.md passed explicitly; no babysitting pattern.
BR-OB-11 High Recording auto-ingest: Meeting recordings must be automatically picked up from wherever they land (Google Drive, OneDrive, email attachment) via a lightweight watcher/script — no manual download or conversion step required before pipeline processing is triggered.
BR-OB-12 High Agentic control flow: System must support specialized agents with explicit handoff conditions, tool selection per node, and traceable control transfer (who invoked which tool, why, and what output was handed to the next agent).
BR-OB-13 Med Nine-assistant platform: All nine specialized assistants (Executive Decision, Finance, Operations, People, Document Pipeline, Knowledge Base, Process Repository, Knowledge Chat, Backlog & Planning) are live with copilot at approval gates, learning cards at task review, and full agentic control flow traceability.

Non-Functional Requirements (Business)
ID Requirement
BNF-01 Privacy & permissions: Enforce least-privilege access; segment sensitive discussions (HR, legal, M&A); support org policies and regional data residency where required.
BNF-02 Accuracy & traceability: Every surfaced claim should tie to sources; confidence or “needs review” flags; no fabrication of approvals or decisions.
BNF-03 Auditability: Immutable logs for ingest jobs, model version, prompt/schema version, approver identity, and publication events.
BNF-04 Scalability: Handle growing volume of meetings and documents; batch and async processing; cost controls via model routing (Haiku vs Sonnet vs Opus).
BNF-05 Availability: Graceful degradation — manual upload and review if AI services are down; queued reprocessing when restored. Monitor Anthropic status page; do not block user workflows on single model unavailability (Opus confirmed down ~10 min Apr 9 2026).
BNF-06 Bounded invocations: No single LLM call spans more than one defined pipeline stage; orchestrator enforces step sequence and can checkpoint / retry individual nodes without re-running the full chain.
BNF-07 Prompt integrity: Prompt and configuration files are loaded from disk at the start of each pipeline run; conversation / thread context is never the sole source of step instructions. Use plan.md as the deterministic instruction set passed to each fresh agent window.
Scope — Phase 1 vs Later

In scope (Phase 1)

  • Meeting-centric ingest, OpenAI Whisper ASR integration (or pre-supplied transcripts), prompt-driven structured extraction
  • Human-in-the-loop review before “trusted” publication
  • Baseline taxonomy, tagging, and searchable repository of structured objects
  • Observability for quality (eval samples, regression checks on schemas)

Deferred / later phases

  • Fully autonomous executive “digital twin” without oversight
  • Replacing formal legal/compliance sign-off
  • Real-time org-wide graph of all systems without integration budget
  • Unbounded cross-tenant SaaS productization (unless explicitly scoped)

Knowledge Architecture

Categorization & linkage

Taxonomy

Domains: Executive, Operations, Clinical, Technology, Finance/Legal. Sub-tags: process, decision, exception, SOP, risk, customer, product.

Knowledge objects

Stable IDs; links to meetings, people, documents; optional graph edges (depends on, supersedes, related_to).

History

Time-aware records; validity windows for “current truth”; archive superseded decisions with rationale.

Learning Model (Operational Definition)
“Learning” is not opaque self-training on raw audio. It is supervised compounding: ingest → extract → validate → publish → retrieve → feedback. Trust tiers evolve with governance.

Acceptance Criteria (Phase 1)
  • A meeting can be ingested end-to-end with metadata, transcript, and structured JSON matching schema v1.
  • Reviewers can edit extracted fields; audit log captures before/after and user identity.
  • Published objects are retrievable by domain/topic with citations to source segments or documents.
  • Trust state machine enforced: draft → reviewed → trusted (no bypass without admin).
  • Role-based access prevents unauthorized access to restricted domains.
  • Model routing documented: OpenAI Whisper (when audio ingested) and Haiku/Sonnet/Opus per step, with model/version or job id logged per ingest pipeline.
  • Each pipeline step (auto-ingest, ASR, extract, dedupe, score, publish) executes as a discrete API / node call; no single monolithic call handles the end-to-end chain.
  • Prompt and config files are re-read from disk at the start of each run via plan.md; deviation from last-run file state triggers a resync warning before the pipeline proceeds.
  • Agent handoff trace is visible per run: source input, active agent, tools used, handoff condition, and final artifact decision path.
Risks and Mitigations
Risk Level Mitigation
Low-quality or biased knowledge compounds High HITL gates, trust tiers, sampling QA, SME ownership per domain, periodic audits.
Hallucinated facts or misquoted decisions High Citations required; confidence flags; forbid publication without review for high-risk domains.
Adoption failure (“another AI summary tool”) perception Med Anchor on workflows (publish, search, training); executive sponsorship; measurable KPIs.
Privacy / regulatory exposure High Data maps, BAAs, residency choices, minimization, retention policies, access reviews.
Over-centralization on one leader’s voice early on Med Explicit roadmap to SME streams; plural approvers; diversity of sources in graph.
Prompt / config drift (stale instructions) High Cue or context file updated without pipeline resync → stale instructions, silent output degradation. Mitigation: hash check on prompt files per run; mandatory resync flag in orchestrator (BR-OB-10 / BNF-07).
Vendor API downtime (Claude / Opus) Med ~10 min Opus outage confirmed Apr 9 2026. Mitigation: monitor Anthropic status page; queue for retry; manual fallback — never block all workflows on one model (reinforce BNF-05).

Strategic outcome: The organization stops re-losing intelligence every quarter. Early phases faithfully capture how leadership thinks and how work really gets done; later phases turn that into shared infrastructure — retrieval, training, process improvement, and resilient execution — without requiring a fully autonomous “corporate brain” on day one. The north star is compounding business intelligence: every meeting makes the next decision cheaper, faster, and better grounded.
Guiding principle
Ambitious vision, pragmatic delivery: ship structured knowledge with governance first; add memory, graph, and advanced reasoning as volume and trust grow. The system should always answer “according to approved sources, on these dates, by these owners” — never pretend to be infallible.
